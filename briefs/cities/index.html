Canopy Intelligence · For Cities & Urban Forest Managers

Know your canopy, meet code, plant for equity

A satellite tree inventory and decision maps — no ground survey.

The problem

You're asked to manage the urban forest as green infrastructure — but the data underneath is thin. A full ground inventory runs $30K–$470K and is out of date the day it's finished. Meanwhile you need answers a static inventory can't give: which neighborhoods need shade most, which trees a wildfire-resiliency code would affect, and where the next dollar of planting should go.

What you get

  • A tree inventory built from satellite — every tree, no field crew, refreshable.
  • A canopy & heat-equity map — find the lowest-canopy, highest-heat blocks.
  • A planting & replacement priority ranking — a defensible "where to focus first."
  • A wildfire-code compliance summary (where relevant) — which trees a defensible-space code would flag.

How it works

Open satellite imagery plus process-based ecological models. No proprietary data required, so the method is reproducible and yours to keep — not locked behind a vendor's paid basemap. Every output is defensible enough to put in front of a council or a funder.
