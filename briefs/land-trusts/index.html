The problem

Stewarding easements means standing behind parcels you can't watch year-round. Small, sub-clearcut disturbances slip past annual site visits, and physical monitoring across thousands of acres is slow and expensive. When something does change, you need to know not just that it happened, but whether it was permitted — judged against the deed.

What you get

  • A monitoring report flagging each forest disturbance with its date.
  • A permitted / review / violation call on each one, adjudicated against the parcel's permitted activity — defensible and reproducible.
  • Coverage that scales across a whole portfolio of easements, not one site at a time.
  • The same method supports improved-forest-management (IFM) carbon verification.

How it works

I detect disturbances from free satellite imagery — no paid-data dependency — and adjudicate each against the governing permission document. Built to run reproducibly, year over year, so monitoring becomes a standing capability instead of a fire drill.
