See where your forest is headed, decades out

A stand-level climate forecast — from open data, no ground survey.

The problem

For woodlands and natural areas, the real question isn't today's inventory — it's where the forest will gain or lose ground. Climate shifts, drought, and pests will move species in and out over the next 50–100 years, but stewardship plans and monitoring budgets are still set on a snapshot of the present. You're managing a system that's moving, with a map that isn't.

What you get

  • A climate-vulnerability map showing where stands are most exposed, broken out by topographic position — the terrain that drives moisture and exposure.
  • A stand-level forecast report — projected composition and biomass 100 years forward under multiple climate pathways, with management scenarios.
  • A defensible basis to target monitoring and planting where the forest is most at risk, instead of spreading effort evenly.

How it works

A process-based forest-dynamics model — the kind used in climate science — run forward over real terrain and driven by open data. This is genuine forecasting of forest change, not a present-day canopy snapshot or a climate-zone lookup. Reproducible, and yours to keep.
