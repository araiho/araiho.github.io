Plant for 2050, not just today's inventory

Every tree scored for future climate and pest pressure.

The problem

A tree you plant today lives for decades — into a climate and pest regime that isn't today's. Species that thrive now may decline under 2050 heat, drought, and pests. Planting and replacement decisions made on current conditions quietly lock in failures you won't see for years, and clients increasingly ask for advice that accounts for it.

What you get

  • Every tree scored for 2050 climate and pest pressure — future performance, not just a present-day inventory.
  • Diversity-aware planting recommendations — species that will still thrive, with over-reliance risk flagged.
  • The dollar value at stake so replacement decisions are defensible to clients.
  • For woodland projects, the same engine extends to stand-level forest forecasting (composition, biomass, scenarios).

How it works

A process-based forest-dynamics model — the kind used in climate science — applied at the scale of individual trees, driven by open data. This is real forecasting, not a hardiness-zone lookup or an AI guess.
